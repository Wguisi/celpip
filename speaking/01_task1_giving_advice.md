# Task 1 — Giving Advice (Dando Conselhos)

## O que é a Task

Você é apresentado a uma situação onde um amigo, familiar ou conhecido precisa de conselho. Você deve dar sugestões práticas e convincentes de forma amigável e estruturada.

- **Prep time:** 30 segundos
- **Speaking time:** 90 segundos (a mais longa da prova junto com Task 7)
- **Formato do prompt:** texto descrevendo a situação + pergunta pedindo seus conselhos
- **Tom:** amigável, prestativo, encorajador

**Exemplo de prompt:**
> "Your friend is planning to start a small business but has never done it before. Give your friend some advice about what he or she should do."

---

## O que os Examinadores Avaliam

| Critério | O que buscam na Task 1 |
|----------|----------------------|
| **Content / Coherence** | Conselhos claramente conectados com razões; progressão lógica de ponto a ponto |
| **Vocabulary** | Variedade de modais (must, ought to, might); verbos específicos de conselho |
| **Listenability** | Tom de voz amigável, naturalidade — não soa como uma lista robótica |
| **Task Fulfillment** | Pelo menos 2–3 conselhos concretos com justificativas; aborda a situação descrita |

---

## Template Estrutural

### Abertura (~15 s)
Estabeleça credibilidade e mostre que entende a situação:

> "Hey [Name], I totally understand that you're dealing with [situation] — it can be really challenging. I've actually [relevant experience] myself, so I have a few suggestions that might really help you out."

### Conselho 1 — Intensidade Forte (~20–25 s)
Use modal de alta intensidade + razão + consequência:

> "**First and foremost, if you want to handle this well, you absolutely must** [action]. **The main reason I suggest this is that** [reason]. **This means that if you do this, you will** [positive outcome]."

### Conselho 2 — Intensidade Média (~20–25 s)
Use modal médio:

> "**Another step you really ought to take is to** [action]. **I say this because** [reason], **and it will help you** [benefit]."

### Conselho 3 — Hipotético / Suave (~20–25 s)
Use condicional hipotético:

> "**One final thing — if I were in your position, I would** [action]. **This is because** [reason], **which could make a big difference in** [outcome]."

### Fechamento (~10 s)
Encoraje sem repetir os conselhos:

> "I hope these suggestions help! I'm sure you'll handle this really well. Feel free to reach out if you need anything else."

---

## Hierarquia de Modais (Obrigatório Variar)

| Intensidade | Modal | Exemplo |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| Alta / Urgente | **must, need to** | "You absolutely must arrive early" |
| Média / Prática | **ought to, should** | "You ought to prepare a budget" |
| Suave / Hipotético | **might, could, would** | "If I were you, I would consider..." |

> **Regra de ouro:** Nunca use "should" mais de uma vez. Use "ought to" como alternativa — soa mais sofisticado e demonstra gama gramatical.

---

## Frases de Vocabulário — Banco de Sentenças

**Para abrir:**
- "I can definitely give some advice here because I have actually [experience]..."
- "Having been through something similar, I understand how difficult this can be..."
- "I know exactly what you're going through, and I have a few ideas that could help."

**Para dar conselhos:**
- "First and foremost, you absolutely must..."
- "One of the most important things you ought to do is..."
- "Another thing worth considering is..."
- "A key step that many people overlook is..."
- "If I were in your shoes, I would definitely..."
- "You might also want to think about..."
- "It would be a good idea to..."
- "Have you considered [verb-ing]? It could really..."

**Para justificar:**
- "The main reason I suggest this is that..."
- "This is important because..."
- "Without this, you risk..."
- "This will help you avoid..."
- "In doing so, you'll be able to..."
- "The benefit of this approach is..."

**Para concluir:**
- "I hope these tips prove useful!"
- "I'm confident you'll figure this out."
- "Best of luck — you've got this!"
- "Don't hesitate to ask if you need more guidance."

---

## O que Fazer nos 30 Segundos de Preparação

1. **Identifique a situação central** (problema, decisão, desafio)
2. **Escolha 3 conselhos distintos** (não variações do mesmo ponto)
3. **Para cada conselho, anote:** ação + razão + resultado
4. **Decida o modal para cada conselho:** must / ought to / would
5. **Prepare a abertura:** como você se conecta ao tema (experiência pessoal)

---

## Erros Comuns e Como Evitar

| Erro | Por que perde pontos | Correção |
|------|---------------------|----------|
| Usar só "should" | Nenhuma variedade modal | Use must → ought to → if I were you... |
| Dar conselhos sem razão | Content fracaco | Sempre: conselho + "because" + consequência |
| Tom muito formal ou distante | Task Fulfillment baixo | É um amigo — use "Hey", "I totally get it", "you've got this" |
| Conselhos genéricos | Content/Coherence baixo | Conecte os conselhos especificamente à situação do prompt |
| Parar antes dos 90 s | Task Fulfillment baixo | Prepare 3 pontos + abertura elaborada + fechamento |
| Repetir o prompt palavra por palavra | Vocabulary baixo | Parafraseie com sinônimos |

## Exemplo de Resposta (CLB 9)

**Prompt:** Seu amigo vai fazer uma entrevista de emprego pela primeira vez.

> "Hey, I totally understand how nervous you must be feeling right now — job interviews can be incredibly stressful. I've actually been through several myself, so let me share a few things that I think could really make a difference.
>
> First and foremost, you absolutely must research the company thoroughly before the interview. The main reason I suggest this is that interviewers almost always ask why you want to work there, and a well-informed answer immediately sets you apart from other candidates.
>
> Another step you really ought to take is to practice answering common questions out loud. I say this because reading answers in your head is very different from actually saying them — practicing helps you sound natural and confident rather than rehearsed.
>
> One final thing — if I were in your position, I would prepare two or three thoughtful questions to ask at the end. This shows genuine interest in the role and leaves a strong final impression.
>
> I hope these suggestions help! I'm confident you'll do a fantastic job — just stay calm and be yourself."
